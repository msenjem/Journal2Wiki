    Clinical Question:



    Does the addition of liraglutide to standard care improve cardiovascular outcomes in patients with type 2 diabetes and high cardiovascular risk?

    Bottom Line:



    Among patients with type 2 diabetes and high cardiovascular risk, liraglutide added to standard care reduced the risk of major cardiovascular events and death from any cause.

    Major Points:





    Guidelines:



    As of this trial's publication, no guidelines have been updated to reflect the results of this study.

    Design:



    Multicenter, double-blind, placebo-controlled, randomized controlled trial.

    Population:



    N=9,340 patients with type 2 diabetes and high cardiovascular risk.

    Interventions:



    Patients were randomized to receive either 1.8 mg (or the maximum tolerated dose) of liraglutide or a placebo once daily as a subcutaneous injection in addition to standard care.

    Outcomes:



    Primary Outcomes:
    - The composite of first occurrence of death from cardiovascular causes, nonfatal myocardial infarction, or nonfatal stroke occurred significantly less in the liraglutide group compared with the placebo group.
    - Death from cardiovascular causes and death from any cause were also lower in the liraglutide group.

    Secondary Outcomes:
    - Nonsignificant reductions were seen with liraglutide compared to placebo for nonfatal myocardial infarction, nonfatal stroke, and hospitalization for heart failure.
    - Fewer microvascular events were observed in the liraglutide group.
    - Adverse events leading to discontinuation of liraglutide were mainly gastrointestinal.
